What is the Business Glossary Generator?

This app automatically creates business glossary terms from the data assets in your Atlan catalog. It reads your tables, views, and columns, then uses AI (Claude) to write clear, business-friendly definitions that your team can review and publish back to Atlan.

The goal is to turn technical metadata into a shared vocabulary that everyone in the organization can understand — without manually writing every definition from scratch.

How It Works

1

Configure

Pick a target glossary, optionally filter by connector or connection, choose which term types to generate, and set how many terms you want.

2

Generate

The app fetches assets from Atlan, ranks them by usage and metadata quality, then sends the top candidates to AI. The AI writes a definition for each asset and classifies it as a Business Term, Metric, or Dimension.

3

Review

All generated terms land in the Review tab as drafts. You can approve, reject, or edit each term. Bulk actions let you approve or reject multiple terms at once.

4

Publish

Approved terms are published to your Atlan glossary with one click. Each term is automatically placed in the correct category (Business Terms, Metrics, or Dimensions).

Term Types

Business Term

A core business concept derived from a data asset. Example: table DIM_CUSTOMER becomes a term called "Customer" with a definition describing what a customer is to the business.

Metric

A measurable value or KPI. Example: table MONTHLY_REVENUE becomes "Monthly Revenue" with a definition explaining the calculation, units, and business targets.

Dimension

A categorical attribute for filtering and grouping. Example: table EMPLOYEE_DIMENSION becomes "Employee" describing how employees are segmented for analysis.

Quick Start

  1. Go to Settings and enter your Atlan API token and Anthropic API key. Click "Test Connection" to verify.
  2. Go to Generate, select your target glossary, pick the term types you want, set the number of terms (1–15), and click Start Generation.
  3. Watch the progress in the activity log. Generation usually finishes in under a minute.
  4. Go to Review Terms to see the generated drafts. Click any term to view and edit its definition.
  5. Approve the terms you like, then click Publish Approved to push them to Atlan.

Tips

  • Upload context files — data dictionaries, docs, or schemas uploaded on the Generate page help the AI write better definitions.
  • Start small — generate 3–5 terms first to check quality before running larger batches.
  • Deduplication is automatic — existing terms in the target glossary and previously generated drafts are skipped.
  • Edit before publishing — click any term on the Review page to refine the name, definition, or examples before it goes to Atlan.
  • Clear All removes every draft from the review queue, so you can start fresh.

Architecture

The app runs on the Atlan Application SDK with these components:

  • FastAPI — serves the web UI and REST API
  • Temporal — orchestrates the multi-step generation workflow with retries and progress tracking
  • Dapr — state store for persisting draft terms between sessions
  • Pyatlan SDK — reads assets from and publishes terms to Atlan
  • Claude (via LiteLLM) — generates term definitions through an OpenAI-compatible proxy
